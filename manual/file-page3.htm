What you can expect

The Customer Relation Management System, also known as CRM, is used as a single location to store all information relevant to a client and all transactions relevant to the client’s dealings with the organisation and third parties involved in the client’s interests. All communications with the client or third parties concerned with the client account are archived in a structured way for easy retrieval and reference.

All information is searcheable and required activities are monitored for necessary follow up to complete pending transactions in a timely manner on the client’s behalf.

Productivity tools like email templates, task and work-flow definitions help increase both compliance and productivity. These predefined standards will greatly reduce training effort for new staff and qualification levels can be lower for most tasks.

Identities are created to hold personal, address and contact information for individuals, trusts or companies and identities are linked to accounts as policy owners, investors, beneficiaries or as a life insured. Identities can be linked between each other to represent a relationship between two individuals or individuals and companies or trusts.

Some features described in this manual require additional packages to be installed. Footnotes will identify the occasions where this is applicable.
